Schematic and PCB Layout Review Checklist

RF Circuits

  • Verify matching and bias networks near RF ICs
  • Include DC-blocking capacitors and RF chokes
  • Confirm correct antenna impedance and labeling
  • Add input/output filters to suppress out-of-band signals

Power Electronics (Buck/Boost/PFC/LLC)

  • Confirm feedback and compensation networks for stability
  • Place output filter caps close to inductor and IC
  • Sense feedback after output filter for accurate voltage
  • Verify loop compensation matches design guidelines
  • Add EMI filters and snubbers where appropriate
  • Use low-ESR caps, fast diodes, correct MOSFETs/inductors
  • Include over-current and over-voltage protections
  • Check high-voltage caps and common-mode chokes in PFC
  • Confirm proper pinout and bootstrap/soft-start components

Signal Integrity & Organization

  • Group schematic sections logically and clearly label
  • Use unique net labels and consistent power rail names
  • Add decoupling caps to every IC’s power pin
  • Annotate all parts with designators and values
  • Follow logical signal flow (“floor plan”)
  • Run ERC/DRC checks and resolve warnings
  • Separate analog/digital blocks and plan grounding strategy

PCB: Crosstalk & Signal Routing

  • Use controlled-impedance and proper spacing for RF/high-speed lines
  • Route as microstrip/stripline; avoid parallel signal runs
  • Length-match and tightly couple differential pairs
  • Keep fast signals away from power/switching nodes
  • Use internal layers and solid reference planes
  • Avoid routing over gaps in ground planes
  • Stitch ground vias along high-speed paths
  • Avoid stubs or right-angle bends

PCB: Grounding & Power Integrity

  • Use solid planes for ground and power
  • Tie separate grounds at a single star point
  • Isolate high-voltage ground but connect at one point
  • Place decoupling caps close with short vias
  • Use wide pours for high-current paths
  • Avoid splitting ground planes under critical nets
  • Stitch ground pours to main ground plane

Shielding Techniques

  • Use grounded copper zones around analog/RF
  • Add guard-ring traces on sensitive nets
  • Apply metal RF shield cans over noisy blocks
  • Shield clocks from analog areas via reference planes

Thermal Management & Creepage/Clearance

  • Use thermal vias under hot components
  • Include copper pours or heatsinks for heat spreading
  • Allow airflow and follow creepage/clearance rules
  • Use coating or slots for tight clearances
  • Clearly mark high-voltage zones

Placement & Partitioning

  • Organize the board by function blocks
  • Cluster power parts separately from analog/digital
  • Keep RF chain compact and short
  • Place connectors at board edges
  • Group analog sensors away from digital noise
  • Follow schematic floor plan in layout
